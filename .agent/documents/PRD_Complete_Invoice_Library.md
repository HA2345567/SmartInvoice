## Part 1: Invoice Types (By Business Function)

### Category A: Standard Transaction Invoices

#### 1. **Sales Invoice** (Standard Invoice)
**Purpose:** Most common invoice - request payment after delivering goods/services  
**When to use:** After product/service delivery  
**Key features:**
- Invoice number
- Itemized list of products/services
- Quantities and prices
- Tax calculations
- Payment terms
- Total amount due

**Industries:** All businesses  
**Template needs:** Clean, professional, easy to read

---

#### 2. **Proforma Invoice** (Preliminary Invoice)
**Purpose:** Provide cost estimate before work begins - NOT a payment request  
**When to use:** 
- Before finalizing a deal
- International trade (customs estimates)
- Client approval processes
- Securing letters of credit

**Key features:**
- Clearly marked "PROFORMA INVOICE"
- Estimated costs
- Payment terms
- Validity period
- "Not for payment" note

**Industries:** International trade, B2B services, large projects  
**Template needs:** Similar to standard but clearly marked as estimate

---

#### 3. **Commercial Invoice**
**Purpose:** Required for international shipments, customs clearance  
**When to use:** ALL international sales/shipments  
**Key features:**
- Detailed product descriptions
- HS codes (Harmonized System codes)
- Country of origin
- Total weight/dimensions
- Shipping terms (Incoterms)
- Declared value for customs

**Industries:** Import/export businesses  
**Template needs:** Formal, detailed, compliant with customs requirements

---

#### 4. **Tax Invoice** (VAT/GST Invoice)
**Purpose:** Document sales with tax breakdown for tax compliance  
**When to use:** Required by law in many countries  
**Key features:**
- Seller's tax ID number
- Buyer's tax ID number
- Tax breakdown (GST/VAT rates)
- Taxable vs non-taxable items
- Total tax amount

**Industries:** All businesses in VAT/GST countries  
**Template needs:** Clear tax breakdown section

---

#### 5. **Credit Note** (Credit Memo)
**Purpose:** Reduce amount owed - refunds, returns, billing errors  
**When to use:**
- Customer returns products
- Overcharge corrections
- Damaged goods
- Discounts applied after invoice

**Key features:**
- Reference to original invoice
- Reason for credit
- Items being credited
- Amount to deduct
- Remaining balance

**Industries:** Retail, wholesale, services  
**Template needs:** Clearly shows negative amount/credit

---

#### 6. **Debit Note** (Debit Memo)
**Purpose:** Increase amount owed - undercharge corrections  
**When to use:**
- Additional charges discovered
- Underbilling correction
- Late fees
- Additional services added

**Key features:**
- Reference to original invoice
- Reason for additional charge
- Additional items/fees
- New total amount

**Industries:** All businesses  
**Template needs:** Clearly shows additional charge

---

#### 7. **Mixed Invoice**
**Purpose:** Combine credits and debits on single invoice  
**When to use:** Multiple corrections needed simultaneously  
**Key features:**
- Original invoice reference
- Credit items
- Debit items
- Net adjustment
- Final total

**Industries:** Complex B2B transactions  
**Template needs:** Clear separation of credits/debits

---

### Category B: Project-Based Invoices

#### 8. **Interim Invoice** (Progress Invoice)
**Purpose:** Bill for work completed at project milestones  
**When to use:**
- Long-term projects (months/years)
- Construction projects
- Software development
- Large consulting engagements

**Key features:**
- Project name/ID
- Milestone completed
- % of total project
- Work period
- Amount invoiced to date
- Remaining balance

**Industries:** Construction, consulting, software, architecture  
**Template needs:** Progress tracking section

---

#### 9. **Final Invoice**
**Purpose:** Request final payment upon project completion  
**When to use:** End of project  
**Key features:**
- Complete project summary
- All interim payments listed
- Final deliverables
- Total project cost
- Amounts paid
- Final balance due

**Industries:** Project-based businesses  
**Template needs:** Comprehensive summary section

---

#### 10. **Retainer Invoice**
**Purpose:** Request advance payment for future services  
**When to use:**
- Monthly retainer agreements
- Prepaid service packages
- Advance payments

**Key features:**
- Retainer period
- Services included
- Hourly/monthly rate
- Payment frequency
- Usage tracking (optional)

**Industries:** Law firms, consultants, agencies  
**Template needs:** Period-based format

---

#### 11. **Timesheet Invoice** (Time-Based Invoice)
**Purpose:** Bill based on hours worked  
**When to use:** Hourly billing, consulting, legal  
**Key features:**
- Detailed time log
- Date and hours per task
- Hourly rate
- Total hours
- Total amount

**Industries:** Lawyers, consultants, freelancers  
**Template needs:** Time tracking table

---

#### 12. **Expense Report Invoice**
**Purpose:** Reimburse business expenses  
**When to use:** Employee expense reimbursement, contractor expenses  
**Key features:**
- List of expenses
- Receipts/documentation
- Expense categories
- Reimbursement amount
- Approval status

**Industries:** All businesses with employees  
**Template needs:** Expense categorization

---

### Category C: Recurring & Specialized Invoices

#### 13. **Recurring Invoice** (Subscription Invoice)
**Purpose:** Automatic billing for ongoing services  
**When to use:**
- Monthly subscriptions
- SaaS products
- Utilities
- Maintenance contracts

**Key features:**
- Subscription period
- Auto-generated
- Next billing date
- Subscription details
- Cancellation policy

**Industries:** SaaS, utilities, gyms, subscriptions  
**Template needs:** Subscription info section

---

#### 14. **Consolidated Invoice**
**Purpose:** Combine multiple transactions into one invoice  
**When to use:**
- Multiple small purchases
- Monthly billing summary
- Simplified billing

**Key features:**
- Transaction date range
- Multiple line items
- Individual transaction dates
- Combined total

**Industries:** Suppliers, service providers  
**Template needs:** Multi-transaction layout

---

#### 15. **Past Due Invoice** (Overdue Notice)
**Purpose:** Remind client of unpaid invoice  
**When to use:** After payment deadline passes  
**Key features:**
- Original invoice details
- Original due date
- Days overdue
- Late fees (if applicable)
- Urgent payment request

**Industries:** All businesses  
**Template needs:** Attention-grabbing, clear urgency

---

#### 16. **Collective Invoice** (Batch Invoice)
**Purpose:** Bill multiple customers for same service  
**When to use:** Regular recurring charges to multiple clients  
**Key features:**
- Service period
- Standard charges
- Individual client sections
- Total for all clients

**Industries:** Property management, utilities  
**Template needs:** Multi-client format

---

#### 17. **Purchase Invoice** (Vendor Invoice)
**Purpose:** Document purchases from suppliers  
**When to use:** When buying goods/services  
**Key features:**
- Vendor details
- Purchase order number
- Items purchased
- Delivery details
- Payment terms

**Industries:** All businesses buying goods  
**Template needs:** Purchase order reference

---

#### 18. **Self-Billing Invoice**
**Purpose:** Buyer creates invoice on behalf of seller  
**When to use:** Agreed self-billing arrangements  
**Key features:**
- Self-billing agreement reference
- Buyer-generated details
- Seller approval section
- Special tax handling

**Industries:** Supply chain, distributors  
**Template needs:** Approval workflow

---

#### 19. **Retail Invoice** (Receipt)
**Purpose:** Point-of-sale transaction record  
**When to use:** Immediate retail sales  
**Key features:**
- Date/time of sale
- Items purchased
- Payment method
- Tax breakdown
- Store location

**Industries:** Retail, restaurants  
**Template needs:** Compact receipt format

---

#### 20. **Account Statement Invoice**
**Purpose:** Summary of all transactions over a period  
**When to use:** End of month account summaries  
**Key features:**
- Opening balance
- All invoices issued
- All payments received
- Closing balance
- Aging summary

**Industries:** All B2B businesses  
**Template needs:** Statement format with running balance

## Part 2: Design Styles (Visual/Aesthetic Categories)

### Style 1: **Corporate Minimal** (Apple, Google, Stripe)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Maximum whitespace
- Thin typography (300-400 weight)
- Monochrome or single accent color
- Clean san-serif fonts
- Subtle lines/separators only

**Best for:** Tech companies, startups, modern businesses  
**Examples:** Apple, Google, Stripe, Notion  
**Color schemes:** Black/white/gray with optional single brand color

---

### Style 2: **Corporate Traditional** (Goldman Sachs, McKinsey, Law Firms)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Serif fonts (Times New Roman, Garamond)
- Centered formal headers
- Heavy borders
- Black and white only
- Traditional table layouts

**Best for:** Law firms, financial services, consulting  
**Examples:** BlackRock, Goldman Sachs, McKinsey  
**Color schemes:** Strictly black and white

---

### Style 3: **Corporate Structured** (Microsoft, IBM, Oracle)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Clear sections with backgrounds
- Sans-serif fonts (Segoe UI, Arial)
- Single brand color accent
- Bordered tables
- Structured grid layout

**Best for:** Enterprise software, B2B services  
**Examples:** Microsoft, Oracle, SAP  
**Color schemes:** Blue, gray, white

---

### Style 4: **E-Commerce Efficient** (Amazon, eBay, Shopify)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Dense information layout
- Bordered sections
- Bold headers
- Clear product listings
- Orange/green accents

**Best for:** E-commerce, retail, wholesale  
**Examples:** Amazon, eBay, Walmart  
**Color schemes:** Orange, green, blue accents

---

### Style 5: **Creative Agency** (Design Studios, Marketing)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Bold typography
- Asymmetric layouts
- Modern fonts (Outfit, Poppins)
- Vibrant colors
- Creative use of space

**Best for:** Design agencies, creative studios, marketing  
**Examples:** Pentagram, IDEO, Frog Design  
**Color schemes:** Vibrant, brand-heavy

---

### Style 6: **Professional Services** (Consulting, Accounting)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Clean but formal
- Sans-serif fonts (Inter, Arial)
- Navy blue or burgundy accents
- Traditional layout with modern touches
- Professional but approachable

**Best for:** Consultants, accountants, professional services  
**Examples:** Deloitte, PwC, Accenture  
**Color schemes:** Navy, burgundy, green

---

### Style 7: **Healthcare/Wellness** (Clinics, Wellness Centers)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Soft, calming colors
- Rounded fonts
- Plenty of whitespace
- Trust-building elements
- Clean and sterile feel

**Best for:** Medical, dental, wellness, therapy  
**Color schemes:** Blue, green, white, soft grays

---

### Style 8: **Construction/Trades** (Contractors, Builders)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Bold, sturdy fonts
- Yellow/orange accents
- Clear itemization
- Practical, no-nonsense layout
- High contrast for readability

**Best for:** Construction, electricians, plumbers  
**Color schemes:** Yellow, orange, black, gray

---

### Style 9: **Restaurant/Hospitality** (Cafes, Hotels)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Warm, inviting colors
- Script or elegant fonts for headers
- Table/seating information
- Friendly, approachable feel

**Best for:** Restaurants, cafes, catering, hotels  
**Color schemes:** Warm tones, browns, reds

---

### Style 10: **Real Estate/Property** (Realtors, Property Management)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Premium feel
- Gold or blue accents
- Property photos (optional)
- Professional luxury aesthetic

**Best for:** Real estate agents, property managers  
**Color schemes:** Gold, navy, white

---

### Style 11: **Education/Nonprofit** (Schools, Charities)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Friendly, accessible
- Clear categorization
- Mission-focused
- Blue or green tones
- Trust-building design

**Best for:** Educational institutions, nonprofits  
**Color schemes:** Blue, green, orange

---

### Style 12: **Automotive** (Car Dealers, Mechanics)
**Visual characteristics:**
- Bold, masculine fonts
- Red or blue accents
- Technical specifications
- VIN/vehicle details prominent

**Best for:** Auto dealers, mechanics, auto services  
**Color schemes:** Red, blue, black
